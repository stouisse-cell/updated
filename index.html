What We Do

The Missing Bridge.

In Morocco's most established markets — Marrakech, Casablanca, Agadir — the path from buyer to property is organised. Agencies are visible. Listings are presented properly. Information is accessible.

In the cities around Rabat — Kénitra, Salé, Témara — the opportunity is equally real. The infrastructure around it is not. Good projects move through personal networks. Buyers spend weeks searching for information that should take days to find. Developers reach a fraction of the audience their projects deserve.

That gap is what we exist to close. Between buyers who need verified information before they commit, and developers whose projects deserve to reach the right audience.

We are the bridge that is missing from this market.

Frequently Asked

Quick answers before you reach out.

  • Yes. Moroccan law allows foreign nationals to own built property and apartments with virtually no restrictions, except for agricultural land. Residency is not required to complete a purchase, and the property is registered in the buyer's name with the Land Registry (Conservation Foncière).

  • On top of the property price, expect roughly 4% registration tax, 1.5% land registry fees, 0.5%–1% notary fees, plus modest filing costs. Altogether, budget around 6%–7% above the purchase price to cover all transfer costs.

  • Daam Sakani is a direct government subsidy of up to 100,000 MAD for first-time buyers acquiring their primary residence. It is also open to Moroccans residing abroad under specific conditions. We provide a full guide explaining the amounts, eligibility, and required documents.

  • Yes. Major Moroccan banks offer dedicated MRE financing of up to 70% loan-to-value, in dirham or in foreign currency. We help you assemble the file, compare offers, negotiate terms, and oversee the entire transaction remotely if you are not in Morocco.
